Gengdu—The Culture of Farming and Studying in Ancient Chinese Society

The land value was endowed with the meaning of guaranteeing wealth and maintaining class in ancient China. This is the deep value that ancient people gave to the land other than the land cultivation. As a traditional agricultural country, China has always had stability in its land policy. How is the land used as a security mechanism for class maintenance? In ancient China, the official-based system is the main structure in society. Success depends on whether or not a person can become a government official. The selection and appointment of officials through the imperial examination is the only way. There is no way to guarantee that one's child will pass the imperial examination. Failing is a high probability event. If a family wants to maintain its class continuously, one way is to increase the probability by having more new births. And this is not even close to success. To allow wealth to be passed on for hundreds of years, farming and schooling have become the best choice. A wealthy family would purchase a large amount of land to collect the rent of it so that the original value can be converted into continuous funds that can provide their children and grandchildren to study. This is a wheel that would never stop. The value of the land is firmly bound to the value of its production. It will not fall and is very stable. In ancient society, the land satisfies people's needs for a stable class. The emperor would declare to the world that he owns all the land as his properties, and that land is his home. It was the projection of one’s power, the real ability a man can manipulate in society.

Teaching computers to speak taboo language, how to use emotional expression may be the easiest bridge between humans and computers. How to establish this emotional connection is the main direction I want to explore. Nonsense vocabulary can express a vague and unclear emotion in the form of sentences. The same is true for human poetry. So can computers produce sentences that make us empathize? Are their answers and emotional catharsis appropriate to us?

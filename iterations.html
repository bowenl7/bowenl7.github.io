Iteractions

Iteration 1 | scroll

After the ideas in form, I decided to rethink the form of the physical body. I choose the scroll as the main key in the instellation because it is a physical interface looks like digital device interface. Audience can play with it and easy to dive into my subject with no time to figure out how to use it. And it is also a "scanner" that can show the ownership of the space. I picked different spaces that are symmetric to lead the audience scroll vertically. My wish is to create multiple racks with different locations physical and vertical.
KEY WORDS: ownership | physical space | scroll | perspective





Iteration 2 | moving bar

Other than a scroll, a moving bar is easer for the audience to move and play with. It is also easier for me to install it on the slides.

KEY WORDS: ownership | physical space | drag and pull | perspective



Feedbacks and Reflection

When it comes to scroll and this behavior, the meaning behinds it need to be cleared. Just barely showing the image is not my purpose. In order to push the message clearer, somehow the interaction need to be changed. For the meaning of the image behind, it is also not clear enough. Why those spaces are special? Who owns the space? Can it really related to the front images? Those are problem I need to solve.

Iteration 3 | stamp

I made a lot of changes this time. I kept the slides part for this time because of its easy to play and no cost of learning. This time I more focused on my own space and what do I owned. I use a magnifier to find and think my own property physically and virtually. The whole concept become clearer and easier to follow for the audience. And stand in a personal perspective gives me opportunity to rethink the concept of ownership.
KEY WORDS: ownership | proof of ownership | authority | physical space | virtual space
